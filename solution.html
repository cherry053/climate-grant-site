Built on what works

Solutions already exist.
The gap is access.

The City of Melbourne's Youth Climate Action Plan has demonstrated that accessible, structured grant funding for young people directly produces measurable local climate outcomes. Youth-led renewable projects. Community water systems. Behaviour change campaigns. It works.

The problem is not the model. The problem is the geography. It exists in Melbourne. It does not exist nationally. Rural youth are not in Melbourne.

The gap: Existing models are limited to cities and already-networked communities. What's needed is not a new idea — it's a national rollout of a proven one, designed from the ground up for rural access.
National Rollout

Three mechanisms.
One system.

01 — Funding

Micro-grants that
actually reach them.

$500–$5,000 micro-grants. One-page application. Plain language. No institutional sponsor required. Available to any young Australian aged 14–25.

Assessed on idea merit and community benefit — not on existing networks or academic affiliation. Larger project grants (up to $50,000) scale up for fully formed proposals, with a supported development pathway to get there.

Delivered through government–community partnerships across all states and territories. Shared funding model means no single state bears the full cost.

💰 Micro-Grants
02 — Recognition

Climate actions earn
transport & funding.

Points for verified actions. Every milestone completed — a workshop run, a planting day organised, a renewable install completed — earns points on a national Climate Action Leaderboard verified by schools and community organisations.

Points convert to transport vouchers (to attend events), additional micro-funding, and pathways into industry mentorships. Transport is the number-one barrier cited by rural youth — making it a reward removes that wall directly.

School-supported verification means a local teacher can confirm participation — no external organisation needed. This unlocks the program for the most isolated communities.

🏆 Reward System
03 — Rural Access

Designed for where
they actually are.

Rural funding multiplier (up to 1.5×) automatically applied to eligible regional applicants — no extra paperwork. More support for those who face higher barriers.

Offline-compatible applications ensure connectivity gaps don't disqualify applicants. Paper-form pathway available at every Australia Post outlet.

A dedicated Rural Climate Mentor Network connects regional youth with experienced practitioners via video call and in-person regional roadshows twice yearly.

🌾 Rural Access
Social Amplification

A digital platform where
the movement becomes visible.

Not just a grant system — a connected national community. Youth share projects, connect across regions, find collaborators, and build a visible national movement around local climate action.
